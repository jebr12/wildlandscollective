Gaze out towards the majestic Andes towering in the distance, feel the cool breeze of Argentino Lake rustling the trees as it blows across the valley, and stand in awe of the sheer natural beauty of Patagonia. Estancia Cristina welcomes you to experience this amazing wonderland from the quiet comforts of a home-away-from-home.

If you’re going to go to the trouble of embarking on a Patagonian trek, you may as well get deep, deep in it. And if remoteness is the new face of luxury, then Estancia Cristina deserves eight or nine stars. From El Calafate you board a small boat for a three-hour ride across the deep blue Lago Argentino and up into its long glacial fingers, ringed by impossibly tall peaks, and when finally you arrive at the Estancia you’ll feel — with some justification — as though you’ve arrived in another world entirely.

One reason to love South American lodges is that they know better than to try to compete with the views — like the best of them, Estancia Cristina’s restrained, almost utilitarian features add to the charm that comes with warm interiors, rich textures, simple fabrics and raw woods. Here three simple structures contain the Estancia’s 12 rooms, each one dominated by a wide picture window oriented towards the mountains.

The facilities are first-rate, the cuisine is satisfyingly well-crafted, and everything’s included aside from the booze. It’s important that the comforts be fully in order back at the ranch, as it were, because the main event here is the Patagonian wilderness. Treks include everything from hikes and horseback rides to bike rides to the Upsala glacier — chances are you’ll need a square meal and a stiff drink at the day’s end. “Go here amazing” - Reviewed January 20, 2012
The boat ride to the estancia will be a highlight of your trip. It goes by glaciers and floating icebergs. The hiking at the estancia was fabulous. Be sure to take the longest hike your ability lets you do as the scenery is so great.

“Quite Haunting” - Reviewed February 29, 2012
Estancia Cristina is both something to visit in its own right and also a stepping off point for the Upsala Glacier.Estancia Cristina is on a very isolated part of Lago Argentina and was built at the beginning of the 20th century by a British family from Southampton that settled there. That they settled there, built this sheep station and had the life they did - all living to a ripe old age - is quite extraordinary. In many ways, it is a God-forsaken, desolate and wind-swept place - but fascinating.
The Upsala glacier is amazing and perhaps the most desolate place I have ever been to in my life - but extraordinary, beautiful and haunting
