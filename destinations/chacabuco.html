Valle Chacabuco, Future Patagonia National Park

Rustic elegance in one of the world's most ambitious conservation projects

Situated in the soon-to-be-opened Patagonia National Park, which has the potential to rival Chile's famed Torres del Paine National Park for its remote wilderness beauty, this estancia is big news in Patagonia. Not only is it one of the largest grasslands-recovery projects in the world — thanks to Conservacion Patagonica, the land trust founded by American philanthropists, Kris and Doug Tompkins — but the hiking, wildlife, and infrastructure are first-class. The six-room, pitched-roof stone building was inspired by the iconic Ahwahnee Lodge in Yosemite and sits in the foothills of the southern Andes. With dark wood walls, cream sofas, and multiple fireplaces, it's ranch elegance at its finest. Plus it's sustainable, with fresh greenhouse vegetables and solar energy. Outside, the park is full of guanacos, endangered huemul deer, flamingos, and pumas: a far cry from the overgrazed estancia Chacabuco was when Tompkins took over in 2006. Estancia guests can get a jump on exploring the more than 600,000 of the property's wild, surrounding acres

Named one of Chile’s best 12 hotels by Outside Magazine and declared by Bruce Babbitt — the US Sectretary of the Interior under Bill Clinton — as "what will be the Yellowstone of South America," the Lodge at Valle Chacabuco combines classic design, rustic yet modest luxury, and unparalleled access to the future Patagonia National Park.

Doug and Kris Tompkins — famed enviornmentalists, conservationists, and outdoor industry entrepreneurial visionaries — had a dream. To not only conserve vast tracks of spectacularly beautiful and ecologically rich Pataganian land, but to also use such conservation as an opportunity for South American citizens to experience and be inspired to further protect the wonders of their natural treasures.

In lives full of accomplishments, perhaps this is Kris and Doug's greatest yet. The foresight, action, and commitment to get the concept from idea to reality has been monumental. Patagonia National Park is the crown jewel in the couple's national park-making efforts.

Conservacion Patagonica — the non-profit organisation set up by Kris and Doug Tompkins — operates the Estancia at Valle Chacabuco as part of its effort to create the future Patagonia National Park. Conservation Patagonica, already controls around 200,000 acres of grassland and forest in the Chacabuco Valley, where the lodge is situated. This acreage abuts two existing reserves, Jeinimeni and Tamango, and the plan is to join them to the Tompkins' land, creating a park of 650,000 acres, about one and a half times the size of Torres del Paine National Park, the best-known and most visited park in Patagonia. The acclaimed eco-lodge is the only fixed accommodation within the existing acreage (though numerous campgrounds are also available for the DIY types).

Completed in 2008, the six-bedroom stone-and-wood Lodge takes inspiration from historic hotels such as Yosemite’s Ahwahnee and Yellowstone’s Old Faithful Lodge, but offers guests a more intimate and personal experience.

Designed and decorated in the style of historic Patagonian estancias, it epitomizes classic, rustic elegance. With handcrafted and largely antique furniture, original photography, and careful detail work, each room has a unique theme drawn from its surroundings. Classic and refined, this beautiful stone lodge was modeled on English architecture in southern Argentina. Patterned tiles, handsome wood and large photographic nature prints foster a warm ambience. There are just six guest rooms, mostly doubles, with some bunks for families.

The Estancia faces towards Mount Tamanguito, the peak behind the main park headquarters. A central living and dining room provides a space for guest to gather at mealtimes and at night. The full bar, wood fireplace and numerous couches allow guests to relax at the end of days, while an entertainment system and large screen offer a chance to view documentaries on the park and Patagonia, as well as other films. From time to time, Conservacion Patagonica staff and visiting experts will give presentations on their work.

Breakfast and dinner is served in an intimate, communal setting, and picnic lunches are provided so guests can explore the park during the day. The breakfast buffet includes toast, eggs, yogurt, cereal, fresh fruit, ham, cheese, jam, honey, juice, coffee and tea. Picnics feature assorted sandwiches, fruit, cookies, and juices. Dinners vary depending on seasonal availability, but generally include an appetizer, salad, main course, and dessert. On occasion, we’ll hold traditional Patagonian asados (barbeques) for all guests and staff. We’re happy to accommodate special eating concerns if you let us know ahead of time.

As an environmentally-sensitive structure, the facility minimizes its impact with energy-efficient construction and design, locally quarried stone, recycled wood, and solar panels.It grows much of its produce in greenhouses located just behind the Estancia, and all meat is grass-fed and raised locally.
